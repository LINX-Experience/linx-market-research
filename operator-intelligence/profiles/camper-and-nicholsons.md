## Business Model

The oldest yacht brokerage in the world (244 years of heritage). Full-service: sales, charter, management, crew agency, new build supervision. Owned by Rodriguez Group since 2001. The scale and corporate structure make this a legacy enterprise, not a startup-friendly boutique. Their charter team has trusted access to the global fleet, including "award-winning superyachts and the most sought-after names in the market."

## Referral Dependency

Mixed. C&N has significant brand recognition and direct marketing capability, but charter brokerage fundamentally relies on advisor and client referrals. Estimated 40-60% referral dependency — lower than boutiques due to brand-driven inbound.

## Top 3 Objections

1. **"We're Camper & Nicholsons. We don't need a referral platform."**
   Response: "You don't need referrals — you need to track and reward the ones you already get. When a C&N charter guest tells their friend to book, that friend often goes to a competitor broker. LINX keeps them in your ecosystem."

2. **"We have our own technology and CRM."**
   Response: "LINX doesn't replace your CRM. It's an acquisition channel. Your existing clients' referrals are tracked, attributed, and converted — without your team doing any work."

3. **"Our corporate structure requires long procurement cycles."**
   Response: "Understood. LINX integrates at the operational level, not the enterprise level. One charter manager can pilot in 30 days. Prove the model, then scale."

## Strategic Note

C&N is a Wave 2 target, not Wave 1. The corporate structure, low approachability, and moderate referral dependency make them a harder sell than boutiques like Morley, Cecil Wright, or Y.CO. However, signing C&N would be a massive credibility signal for the yacht vertical. Consider approaching after 2-3 boutique yacht operators are live.
