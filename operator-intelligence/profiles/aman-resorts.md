**Breakdown notes:**
- Aman is aspirational for LINX's brand but NOT an ideal ICP fit for the business model.
- Revenue too large, referral dependency too low, commission arbitrage minimal.
- The real value of Aman would be brand signal — "If LINX is good enough for Aman..." But the path to get there is long.
- Better as a Phase 2/3 target after LINX has proven with mid-market operators.

## Psychology & Approach

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Archetype** | The Brand Fortress — absolute control over brand experience, no compromises |
| **Motivation** | Expansion (12 properties in pipeline, cruise launch, residential). Maintaining brand integrity during rapid growth. |
| **Top 3 objections** | 1. "We don't need another distribution channel — our guests find us." 2. "Our brand cannot appear alongside other operators in a marketplace." 3. "We control every touchpoint — a referral platform adds an uncontrolled layer." |
| **Approach strategy** | Do NOT approach as a distribution channel. Position LINX as a "private membership community where Aman guests share experiences with their peers." Frame as: "Your Amanjunkies already recommend you to their networks — LINX is the private community where that happens naturally." Emphasize LINX's exclusivity and invite-only model. This is a long-game, relationship-first approach. |
| **Intro path** | Through ultra-luxury hospitality connections, Virtuoso network, or direct to Aman's partnerships team. Not through Doronin directly. |
| **Timing** | Late — after LINX has significant traction and brand credibility. Phase 2/3 target. |
