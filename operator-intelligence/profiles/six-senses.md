## Archetype: Corporate Wellness Luxury

Six Senses is a brand managed by IHG, not an independent operator. The properties are owned by third-party developers and managed under the Six Senses brand. This means: (1) commission decisions are made at IHG corporate level, not property level; (2) distribution is handled through IHG's massive loyalty/booking infrastructure; (3) referral dependency is low because IHG fills rooms through its own channels.

---

## LINX Value Proposition

**Pitch:** Limited. IHG's corporate structure makes Six Senses a poor early-stage LINX target. The value proposition works better for independent luxury properties, not brand-managed hotels.

**If forced to pitch:** "Six Senses properties that opt out of IHG One Rewards (several do) are the exception — they need alternative distribution. For those properties specifically, LINX could provide trust-based referral tracking at lower commission than travel advisor networks."

## Strategic Note

Six Senses is profiled for completeness and competitive intelligence, not for near-term outreach. Their aggressive 2026 expansion (5+ new properties) makes them a market force in luxury wellness. The "Six Senses Place" private members concept in London is worth monitoring — if they build community features, it could eventually compete with LINX's discovery model in the wellness vertical. Deprioritize for operator outreach. Use as market intelligence reference.
