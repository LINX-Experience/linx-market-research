## Business Model

Asilia operates 17 bespoke camps and lodges across three countries: Tanzania (12 properties including Jabali Ridge, Namiri Plains, Sayari, Oliver's Camp), Kenya (4 properties including Rekero, Naboisho, Encounter Mara), and Uganda (Erebero Hills, opening 2026). Their "Reserve" tier (Jabali Ridge, Namiri Plains, Sayari, Naboisho, Erebero Hills) is ultra-luxury.

Backed by impact investors Norfund (Norwegian sovereign wealth fund) and IFU (Danish Investment Fund), who jointly provided $9M during COVID. Also partnered with African Wildlife Capital for conservation bonds. B Corp certified through Nawiri Group.

## Referral Dependency

Safari operators in East Africa are heavily referral-dependent. Asilia's distribution mixes direct booking, travel advisor referrals, and tour operator partnerships. Given the remote, high-end nature of their camps, estimated 60-75% referral dependency through advisors and word-of-mouth.

## Strategic Note

Asilia is the largest safari operator profiled so far (17 properties, $66.9M revenue). Their scale is both an advantage (high volume) and a challenge (enterprise sales cycle, investor oversight). The revenue exceeds the ICP sweet spot ($5-25M), which lowers the score. However, the conservation alignment, B Corp certification, and ultra-luxury positioning make them a strong cultural fit for LINX. Consider as a Wave 2 target after signing smaller operators (Angama, Great Plains) to prove the safari vertical.
