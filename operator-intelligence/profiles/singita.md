## Distribution & Commission

| Channel | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Primary distribution** | Travel advisors/agents (primary), direct bookings (secondary) |
| **Commission structure** | East African safari model: agents receive discounted rates (not direct commission), mark up 10-20% to clients. Some properties use net rate model. |
| **Effective agent commission** | 10-20% (via markup on net rates) |
| **Referral dependency** | HIGH (~70-80%). Singita's ultra-luxury positioning means most bookings come through trusted safari specialists and word-of-mouth from past guests. Limited mass-market presence. |
| **Key channels** | Safari specialist agents (Rhino Africa, &Beyond partners, etc.), Virtuoso advisors, direct via singita.com, repeat guests |
| **LINX rate** | 10% (safari vertical) |
| **LINX savings** | 0-10 percentage points. In net-rate models LINX could be competitive; in markup models the savings flow differently. |
| **Commission arbitrage** | MODERATE — depends on how Singita structures agent deals. Net rate model means LINX value is in lead quality, not pure commission savings. |

## Psychology & Approach

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Archetype** | The Conservation Purist — luxury as a vehicle for conservation, not the other way around |
| **Motivation** | Protecting concessions and conservation work; maintaining exclusivity; growing sustainably without diluting brand |
| **Top 3 objections** | 1. "We don't need more guests — we need the RIGHT guests." 2. "Our agent relationships are decades deep — why add a platform?" 3. "How do you protect our brand positioning in a 'referral network'?" |
