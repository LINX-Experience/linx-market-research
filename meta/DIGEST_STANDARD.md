# LINX Daily Intelligence Digest — Format Standard v1.0

> **This is the authoritative format for all daily digests.**
> **Every agent producing a digest MUST follow this standard.**
> **Violation = rewrite. No exceptions.**

---

## Core Principles

1. **5W1H on every signal.** WHO / WHAT / WHEN / WHERE / WHY / HOW. If unknown, write "Unknown." NEVER fabricate.
2. **Quartz-style depth.** Not a bullet list — a morning briefing that makes you smarter. Every paragraph earns its space.
3. **Cross-venture coverage.** LINX + AXIOM + Stein Studios + personal brand. The founder runs three ventures.
4. **5-8 minute read.** Dense but readable. 1,500-2,500 words.
5. **Zero hallucination.** Every claim sourced. If WebSearch returns nothing, say "Source not confirmed" or "No recent data found."

## Structure (Follow This Exact Order)

### 1. HEADER
```
# LINX Daily Intelligence Brief — {Month Day, Year}
> {X} days until Monaco GP | Day {N} of Research Engine
```

### 2. CRITICAL ALERTS (if any)
Red-flag items requiring same-day action. 2-3 sentences max per alert.

### 3. THE LEAD STORY (3-5 paragraphs)
The single most important thing that happened in the last 24 hours across all ventures. Full 5W1H treatment. Context. Analysis. Implications.

Format:
```
## The Lead: {Headline}

**WHO:** {specific entity}
**WHAT:** {what happened — 1-2 sentences, sourced}
**WHEN:** {date or timeframe}
**WHERE:** {geography, market segment}
**WHY:** {underlying driver — not just "because they announced it" but the strategic reason}
**HOW this affects us:** {specific mechanism connecting this to LINX / AXIOM / Studios}

{2-3 paragraphs of Quartz-style analysis: the big picture, what it means for the industry, 
what it means for Stein's ventures specifically, what the second-order effects are}
```

### 4. LINX INTELLIGENCE (3-5 signals)
Market signals directly relevant to LINX GTM, product, or positioning.

Each signal gets:
```
### {Signal Headline}

**WHO:** / **WHAT:** / **WHEN:** / **WHERE:** / **WHY:** / **HOW:**

**Confidence:** CONFIRMED / LIKELY / UNVERIFIED
**Urgency:** CRITICAL / HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW

{1-2 paragraphs: what this means for LINX specifically, what action to take}
```

### 5. OPERATOR & COMPETITIVE WATCH
New operator profiles completed, competitive moves detected, ICP pipeline updates.

Each item gets the 5W1H treatment (can be shorter — 1 paragraph per item for secondary signals).

## Quality Gates

Before finalizing any digest:

1. **Source check:** Does every factual claim have a URL or publication name? If not, mark "Source not confirmed."
2. **5W1H check:** Does every signal have all 6 elements? If any are unknown, explicitly say "Unknown."
3. **Hallucination check:** Did WebSearch actually return this information? If you're drawing from training data rather than live search, mark "Based on prior knowledge — verify current status."
4. **Length check:** Is this 1,500-2,500 words? If under 1,000, it's too thin. If over 3,000, cut the lowest-urgency signals.
5. **Action check:** Does every HIGH/CRITICAL signal have a specific recommended action? Not "monitor" — an actual next step.
6. **Cross-venture check:** Does the digest cover at least 2 of the 3 ventures (LINX, AXIOM, Studios)?

---

## Tone

- Authoritative but conversational (Quartz, not Bloomberg Terminal)
- Direct. No hedging. If you're uncertain, say so explicitly rather than softening language.
- "Here's what happened and here's why you should care" — every paragraph
- No emojis. No exclamation points. No "exciting" or "amazing."
- Challenge assumptions. If a data point contradicts prior research, flag the contradiction.

---

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER Do These)

- Bullet-only digests with no analysis
- Signals without sources
- "Monitor this" without a specific trigger for action
- Covering only LINX when AXIOM or Studios news exists
- Inventing URLs or data points
- Presenting training-data knowledge as live research without flagging it
- Skipping the Bottleneck Check to avoid uncomfortable truths
- Writing "no significant news" without actually searching
